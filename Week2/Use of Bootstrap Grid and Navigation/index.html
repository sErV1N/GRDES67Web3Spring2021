Laquerware and Its Various Forms

Red lacquer wood bowl from the Chinese Hemudu culture dated to 4000–5000 BC, the oldest such piece ever found.
A lacquerware painting from the Jingmen Tomb (荊門楚墓; Jīngmén chǔ mù) of the State of Chu (704–223 BC), depicting men wearing precursors to Hanfu (i.e. traditional silk dress) and riding in a two-horsed chariot.
Carved lacquer cup with a poem composed by the Qianlong Emperor (1735–1796), Qing dynasty.
